Corn Stuffed Peppers

Recipe courtesy of Eric Bearden, 2004 

6 sweet bell peppers, red, yellow, orange, or a combination

2 T butter

½ cup minced celery

½ cup minced onions

4 ears of fresh corn, kernels cut off the cob

½ to 1 whole diced jalapeno depending on preference of heat

minced garlic, several cloves

3 eggs, slightly beaten

salt and pepper to taste

1½ cups of bread crumbs

1½ cups of cheese-

 

Swiss is actually good, but cheddar, mozzarella, or pepper jack would be good choices too.  A combo of a few different kinds works well.  

 

Optional:  3 medium tomatoes, minced and seeded.  Or chicken broth or vegetable broth would be good to keep the stuffing moist.  I would pour the broth over the peppers about halfway through the baking process.  Could add black beans and rice for heartier peppers, or serve this on the side.  I think diced green chilies would be good to add as well.  Finally, some type of seeds would be a good addition too- sunflower, or pinons.

 

Slice the tops off the peppers.  Use peppers that will stand upright in a casserole dish.  Rub oil on the peppers inside and out, and then blanch in boiling water for 5 minutes.  Then cool them down to stop the cooking process and place in a casserole dish with ½ inch of water.  Save the tops of the peppers as well.

 

Saute the onion, garlic, and celery in the butter until translucent.  In a large bowl add the onion, celery, and garlic to the corn and  jalapeno.  Toss in the breadcrumbs and salt and pepper.  Add the eggs and mix well.  (Optional:  This is a good time to add the rice and black beans if you want.)  Add some of the cheese into the mixture but save some for the tops of the peppers later.  

 

Stuff the peppers- mounding the tops.  Cover with foil and bake at 350 degrees for 1 hour, the last 10 minutes take the foil off and add the cheese to the tops of the peppers and replace the lids.
